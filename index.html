Lukáš Kyjánek


kyjanek@ufal.mff.cuni.cz

I am a PhD student at the Institute of Formal and Applied Linguistics, Faculty of Mathematics and Physics, Charles University.

My research interests are:

  • word-formation with focus on derivational morphology and semantics in derivations, including meanings of morphological categories;
  • computer lexicography with focus on resources capturing and processing word-formation, but not exclusively;
  • dialectometry with focus on mutual intelligibility of closely related languages.
I prefer to exploit statistics and machine learning for dealing with the mentioned topics of (computational) linguistics.

PROJECTS

Formalisation of word-formation meanings in language resources

I formalise derivational meanings, label them in language resources of several languages and compare competition in them within individual languages as well as across the languages.
Dissertation Thesis
(work in progress)

A data-based approach to competition in word-formation

I lead a team researching competition in word-formation processes and means employed to express the meanings of diminutiveness and femaleness in seven European languages.
START Research Programme, CUNI
(principal investigator, 2021-2023)

Project webpage
Word-formation structure of Czech words

I am part of a team which focuses on linguistic research into the word-formation structure of Czech words using specialized language resources and tools and large language corpora.
GAČR Grant, CUNI MFF UFAL
(team member, 2019-2021)

Project webpage


Universal Derivations

I develop the collection of harmonised lexical networks capturing word-formation in a cross-linguistically consistent annotation scheme for many languages.
Master Thesis; Data resource related to Grants in which I am involved

Project webpage

DeriNet

I help to develop a lexical, derivational network for Czech. It includes, for example, enlargement of relations captured, preparation of manual annotations, and semantic labelling.
Data resource related to Grants in which I am involved

Project webpage
Derivational networks for multiple languages

With Jonáš Vidra and Tomáš Musil, we focus on developing derivational networks for multiple languages using machine learning and cross-linguistical transfer of knowledge.
GAUK Grant, CUNI
(team member, 2019-2021)



Semantic labels into DeriNet

I carried out first experiments with labelling of derivational meanings in DeriNet using machine learning methods. As a result, the data contains five labels in DeriNet 2.0.
Student's Faculty Grant, MFF CUNI (principal investigator, 2019)

GitHub repository

Foreign words recognition

I developed a rule-based tool for the recognition of borrowed/foreign/loan words in the Czech language. Used rules are based on the study of so far published linguistic papers.
Student's Faculty Grant, MFF CUNI (principal investigator, 2018)

GitHub repository
Mutual Intelligibility between West-Slavic Languages

With Jiří Haviger, we focus on measurement of mutual intelligibility between West-Slavic languages, especially using conditional entropy and methods originated from dialectometry.
Bachelor Thesis; ongoing research without grant support

PRESENTATIONS

PRESENTATIONS

  • .pdf
    2021. Derivational meaning in language resources.
    UFAL PhD Conference.
    Description: Presentation of work on my dissertation research topic in the first year of my studies. Derivational meanings are introduced; one experiment with labelling derivational meanings in Czech is presented; a plan for using unsupervised methods is mentioned; challenges and their solutions are discussed.
  • .pdf
    2020. Harmonizace jazykových zdrojů zachycujících slovotvorbu různých jazyků.
    Master Thesis defence.
    Description: The final presentation of results from my Master Thesis. It summarises word-formation and the existing resources capturing word-formation, and it describes the harmonisation process that I developed for unifying the existing resources into the common data structure and annotation scheme. Some statistical properties of the resulting Universal Derivations (v1.0) collection are also presented.
  • .pdf
    2019. Universal Derivations Kickoff: A Collection of Harmonized Derivational Resources for Eleven Languages.
    DeriMo Workshop.
    Description: The opening presentation of the work in progress in the field of harmonising data resources that capture word-formation of different languages (presented by Zdeněk Žabokrtský). The most important information is introduced: the current situation on data resources, our design decisions of the harmonisation process, the harmonisation process, the resulting Universal Derivations collection (v0.5).
  • .pdf
    2019. Introducing Semantic Labels into the DeriNet Network.
    Slovko Conference.
    Description: Presentation of the pilot work on labelling five derivational meanings in the Czech word-formation resource DeriNet. The resource is introduced first, and then derivational meanings, the machine-learning approach to label them and the evaluation of the approach are presented.
  • .pdf
    2018. The Measurement of Mutual Intelligibility between West-Slavic Languages.
    UFAL Seminar.
    Description: Presentation of the work on measuring mutual intelligibility among Czech, Slovak and Polish, which was published in the Journal of Quantitative Linguistics. The phenomenon of mutual intelligibility and its sociolinguistic measurements are presented first, and then the computational approach and its results are summarised.
  • .pdf
    2017. Měření jazykové vzdálenosti mezi západoslovanskými jazyky.
    Mezinárodní setkání mladých lingvistů (Student a věda).
    Description: Presentation of the case study on measuring mutual intelligibility among Czech, Slovak and Polish resulted from my Bachelor thesis. The methodology of the computational approach to the measurement is presented as well as the exploited data. Results are illustrated on one genre/style, but the relation of all above-mentioned languages.
  • .pdf
    2017. Semikomunikace v konceptu komunikace.
    Bachelor Thesis defence.
    Description: The final presentation of results from my Bachelor Thesis. It describes the phenomenon of so-called semicommunication, or mutual intelligibility in other words, presents the methodology of computational approaches to the measurement of the phenomenon, and answers the reviewer's and supervisor's questions.
